üìà Model Performance Metrics

üìà ROC Curve

What it shows: The ROC (Receiver Operating Characteristic) Curve illustrates the trade-off between True Positive Rate (sensitivity) and False Positive Rate (1-specificity) at different classification thresholds.

Why it matters: A curve closer to the top-left corner indicates better model performance. The Area Under Curve (AUC) metric ranges from 0 to 1, where 1.0 is perfect classification and 0.5 is random guessing.

üéØ Key Metric: AUC score tells us the probability that the model ranks a random spam email higher than a random legitimate email.

üéØ Confusion Matrix

What it shows: A 2√ó2 matrix showing the model's classification results: True Positives (TP), True Negatives (TN), False Positives (FP), and False Negatives (FN).

Why it matters: Helps understand where the model makes mistakes. FN (missed spam) is costly, while FP (false alarms) reduces user trust.

üìä Precision‚ÄìRecall Curve

What it shows: The relationship between Precision (how many predicted spam are actually spam) and Recall (how many actual spam are detected).

Why it matters: Crucial for imbalanced datasets. High precision means fewer false alarms; high recall means catching more threats. The ideal point is the top-right corner.

‚öôÔ∏è Precision = TP/(TP+FP) | Recall = TP/(TP+FN). Choose threshold based on acceptable false alarm rate.
